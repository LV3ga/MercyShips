Signif. codes:  0 '***' 0.001 '**' 0.01 '*' 0.05 '.' 0.1 ' ' 1

EDF IS HOW "WIGGLY" THE SMOOTH FUNCTION OF A PREDICTOR IS
1 = STRAIGHT LINE, 2 = QUADRATIC
REF.DF AND F ARE TEST STATISTICS USED IN ANOVA TESTS FOR OVERALL SIGNIFICANCE OF THE SMOOTH
WHAT IS A "SIGNIFICANT SMOOTH TERM" -> ONE WHERE YOU CANNOT DRAW A HORIZONTAL LINE THROUGH
THE 95% CI ON THE PLOT 
Approximate significance of smooth terms:
                edf Ref.df      F  p-value
s(weight)     6.254  7.439 20.909  < 2e-16 ***
s(rpm)        7.499  8.285  8.534 2.07e-09 ***
s(price)      2.681  3.421  1.678    0.155
s(comp.ratio) 1.000  1.001 18.923 2.22e-05 ***
s(width)      1.001  1.001  0.357    0.551

SO THE INTERPRETATION HERE IS
	WEIGHT IS SIGNIFICANT (HORIZONTAL LINE DOESN'T FIT IN 95% CI ON PLOT) AND NONLINEAR
	RPM IS SIGNIFICANT AND NONLINEAR
	PRICE IS NOT SIGNIFICANT AND NONLINEAR
	COMP.RATIO IS SIGNIFICANT AND LINEAR
	WIDTH IS NOT SIGNIFICANT AND LINEAR

WITH GAMS, YOU ALSO HAVE TO WATCH OUT FOR CONCURVITY.

WHAT'S CONCURVITY? FIRST, LET'S RECALL COLINEARITY FROM LINEAR MODELS
IF TWO PREDICTOR VARIABLES HAVE A STRONG LINEAR RELATIONSHIP WITH EACH OTHER,
IT CAN BE DIFFICULT TO FIT THE MODEL, BC THE OUTCOME VARIABLE COULD BE RESPONDING
TO EITHER ONE. THIS CAN RESULT IN POORLY FIT MODELS WITH LARGE CONFIDENCE INTERVALS

(SEE COLINEARITY.png)

WITH GAMS, VARIABLES COULD HAVE CONVURVITY, MEANING ONE IS A SMOOTH CURVE OF ANOTHER.
LIKE COLLINEARITY, THIS CAN LEAD TO POORLY FIT MODELS WITH LARGE CONFIDENCE INTERVALS.
THIS IS BEST UNDERSTOOD BY VIEWING THE PNG BELOW.

(SEE CONCURVITY1.png)
